--- layout: post title: 'R and "The Art of R Programming: A Tour of Statistical Software Design"' comments: true categories: [] tags: - R - statistics status: publish type: post published: true meta: _edit_last: '1' --- I need to do a little simple statistical analysis (hopefully simple) in the near future and was looking around for the best tool to use. I am looking at R and Python and thinking that R is the winner at least for now. I picked up (Kindle version) "The Art of R Programming: A Tour of Statistical Software Design" and have been impressed with it and with R. R is particularly new user friendly in a number of ways including that a variety of data sets are packaged in which can be used for examples and they are just plain interesting to mess with. I used R a few times years ago and but had forgotten everything about it. I'm hoping that this experience will stick with me this time.
